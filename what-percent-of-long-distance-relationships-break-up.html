What percent of long-distance relationships break up?

It's estimated that about 50% of all long-distance relationships end in breakups. That means that out of every 10 long-distance relationships, 5 will end in a breakup. The main reason for this is because long-distance relationships are very difficult to maintain. There are a lot of challenges that come with being in a long-distance relationship, and not everyone is able to overcome them. One of the biggest challenges is the lack of physical intimacy. When you are in a long-distance relationship, you are not able to see each other as often as you'd like, and when you do see each other, it's usually just for a short period of time. This can make it difficult to maintain the physical intimacy that's so important in a relationship. Another challenge is the lack of communication. When you are not able to see each other often, it can be easy to forget to communicate with each other about what's going on in your lives. This can lead to misunderstandings and arguments. Long-distance relationships are also more prone to jealousy and trust issues because you are not able to see what the other person is doing when you are not together. If one person starts dating someone else, it can be very difficult for the other person to trust them again. These are just some of the challenges that come with being in a long-distance relationship. If you are thinking about starting one, or if you are already in one, it's important to be aware of these challenges so that you can try to overcome them

How Do I Stop overthinking in a long-distance relationship?

It's normal to overthink things in a long-distance relationship. After all, you are not able to see each other every day and you are relying on communication to stay connected. The key is to not let your thoughts spiral out of control. If you find yourself overthinking things, here are a few tips to help you stop: 1. Talk to your partner about what's on your mind. 2. Write down your thoughts in a journal or on a piece of paper. 3. Distract yourself with other activities. 4. Talk to a friend or family member about your concerns. 5. Seek professional help if your overthinking is causing anxiety or depression
