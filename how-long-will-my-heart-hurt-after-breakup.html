How long will my heart hurt after breakup?

Your heart will hurt after a breakup, but the pain will eventually go away. The length of time it takes for your heart to heal depends on many factors, such as the severity of the breakup, how long you were together, and whether you were the one who ended the relationship. If you were the one who was dumped, it's likely that your heart will hurt more and take longer to heal. But even if you were the dumper, you'll still experience some pain and sadness. There are a few things you can do to speed up the healing process. One is to keep yourself busy. Fill your time with activities that make you happy and help take your mind off of your ex. Another is to talk to your friends and family about what happened. They can offer support and help you see things from a different perspective. Finally, try to focus on the positive aspects of your life. This can be difficult after a breakup, but it's important to remember that there are still good things in your life even if your relationship has ended. The pain from a breakup will eventually go away, but it might take some time. Just remember that you are not alone in this and that there are people who care about you and want to help you through this tough time.

How do you know if the dumper wants you back?
