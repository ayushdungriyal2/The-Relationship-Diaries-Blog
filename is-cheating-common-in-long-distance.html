Is cheating common in long-distance?

There is no easy answer to this question, as it depends on a variety of factors. However, studies have shown that cheating is more common in long-distance relationships than in traditional relationships. One reason for this is that long-distance relationships require more trust than traditional relationships. When you are not able to see your partner on a regular basis, it's easy to start questioning their fidelity. Another reason why cheating is more common in long-distance relationships is because of the lack of physical intimacy. This can often lead one or both partners to seek out physical intimacy with someone else. Finally, long-distance relationships can be more challenging emotionally, which can lead to one or both partners seeking emotional intimacy with someone else. If you are in a long-distance relationship, it's important to communicate openly and honestly with your partner about your needs and concerns. Cheating is never acceptable, but it's also important to remember that everyone makes mistakes. If you are feeling tempted to cheat, talk to your partner about it first. They might be able to provide the support you need to stay faithful.

What are red flags in a long-distance relationship?

There are a few key things to look out for that may indicate trouble in a long-distance relationship. One of the most important is communication. If you and your partner are not communicating regularly, it can be a sign that something is wrong. Another red flag is if you feel like you are always the one initiating contact. If your partner never calls or texts you first, it may be a sign that they're not as invested in the relationship as you are. Additionally, if you find yourself making excuses for your partner's behavior or feeling like you are always walking on eggshells, it's time to have a serious conversation about what's going on. Finally, if you are starting to feel like your partner is more like a roommate than a romantic partner, it may be time to reassess the relationship. Long-distance relationships can be tough, but they're also worth fighting for if both people are committed to making it work. If you are seeing any of these red flags, don't ignore them. Talk to your partner about what's going on and see if there's anything that can be done to improve the situation.
